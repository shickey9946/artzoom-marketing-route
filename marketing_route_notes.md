This transit-optimized, rapid-succession route is designed to get all 20 posters up efficiently using the Red Line, the 66 Bus, the Green Line (B and E branches), and the Green Line Extension (GLX).

Here is your step-by-step guerrilla marketing blueprint, starting and ending at 11 Waterhouse Street.

## **Marketing Route**

### **Phase 1: The Somerville Launch**

* **Stop 1: Teele Square Border**  
  * *Travel from 11 Waterhouse St:* 3-minute walk.  
  * *Placement:* Traffic light post at the Broadway and Curtis Street intersection.  
* **Stop 2: Davis Square**  
  * *Travel time:* 10-minute walk down Holland Street.  
  * *Placement:* The large metal utility control box directly outside the main Davis T Station entrance. This catches commuters heading underground.

### **Phase 2: The Harvard & Allston Pipeline**

* **Stop 3: Eliot Street Bus Stop (Harvard Sq)**  
  * *Travel time:* 10 minutes via the Red Line (Davis to Harvard).  
  * *Placement:* On the exterior metal frame of the bus shelter. People waiting for the bus have high dwell time.  
* **Stop 4: Harvard Art Museum**  
  * *Travel time:* 5-minute walk over to Quincy Street.  
  * *Placement:* The crosswalk button pole directly across from the museum entrance. It targets your exact art-enthusiast demographic.  
* **Stop 5: Union Square (Allston)**  
  * *Travel time:* 12 minutes via the 66 Bus from Harvard Square to the Union Square/Brighton Ave stop.  
  * *Placement:* Light pole near the main Cambridge Street and Brighton Ave convergence, right where pedestrians wait to cross.  
* **Stop 6: Harvard Ave B-Line Platform**  
  * *Travel time:* 5-minute walk down Harvard Ave.  
  * *Placement:* The traffic light post directly on the concrete pedestrian street island. Commuters waiting for the B-Line train will look right at it.

### **Phase 3: The Fenway Art Cluster**

* **Stop 7: Kenmore T Stop**  
  * *Travel time:* 10 minutes via the Green Line B-Branch (Harvard Ave to Kenmore).  
  * *Placement:* A utility box right outside the main bus loop/subway entrance.  
* **Stop 8: Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum**  
  * *Travel time:* 12-minute walk down Brookline Ave and Evans Way.  
  * *Placement:* Street sign pole on the sidewalk perimeter facing Evans Way park, capturing the museum queue crowd.  
* **Stop 9: MassArt**  
  * *Travel time:* 3-minute walk over to Huntington Ave.  
  * *Placement:* The crosswalk pole directly in front of the main MassArt campus building. Excellent spot for design and art students.  
* **Stop 10: Museum of Fine Arts (MFA)**  
  * *Travel time:* 4-minute walk down Huntington Ave.  
  * *Placement:* Light post immediately adjacent to the MFA Green Line E-Branch stop.

### **Phase 4: Back Bay & Downtown**

* **Stop 11: Boston Public Library (Copley)**

  * *Placement:* Heavy traffic control box at the busy corner of Boylston Street and Dartmouth Street.  
* **Stop 12: Boston Common (Arlington Side)**  
  * *Travel time:* 5-minute walk down Boylston Street.  
  * *Placement:* A perimeter utility pole right at the corner of the park, directly across from the Arlington T exit.  
* **Stop 13: Boston Common (Park Street Side)**  
  * *Travel time:* 8-minute walk across the Tremont Street side of the Common.  
  * *Placement:* The metal traffic control box situated right next to the historic Park Street T station kiosks.  
* **Stop 14: Post Office Square**  
  * *Travel time:* 8-minute walk down Winter/Summer Street into the Financial District.  
  * *Placement:* A perimeter streetlamp post on the sidewalk bordering the central park area.  
* **Stop 15: Christopher Columbus Waterfront Park**  
  * *Travel time:* 8-minute walk towards Atlantic Ave and the Greenway.  
  * *Placement:* A prominent signpost or light pole near the park's main Atlantic Ave brick pavilion entrance.  
* **Stop 16: Paul Revere Statue (North End)**  
  * *Travel time:* 7-minute walk up into the North End along Hanover Street.  
  * *Placement:* A streetlamp post on Hanover Street directly outside the entrance to the Paul Revere Mall courtyard.

### **Phase 5: The Tufts & Home Stretch**

* **Stop 17: Tufts University (Lower Campus / Joyce Cummings Center)**  
  * *Travel time:* 20 minutes. Walk 5 minutes to Haymarket Station, then take the Green Line Extension (GLX) Med-Hill branch straight to the Tufts University station.  
  * *Placement:* A light pole directly outside the exit of the Tufts GLX station.  
* **Stop 18: Tufts University (Upper Campus)**  
  * *Travel time:* 5-minute walk up the campus hill.  
  * *Placement:* A public outdoor bulletin board or utility post near the Mayer Campus Center.  
* **Stop 19: Nathan Tufts Park (Powderhouse Square)**  
  * *Travel time:* 8-minute walk down College Ave to the rotary.  
  * *Placement:* A signpost near the main walking path entrance closest to the historic stone powder house.  
* **Stop 20: Teele Square Approach**  
  * *Travel time:* 8-minute walk back toward your neighborhood.  
  * *Placement:* A utility pole on the final stretch of your walk home to anchor the perimeter of your local neighborhood crowd.
