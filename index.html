Guerrilla Hanging Rules:

  • Use heavy-duty transparent packing tape (wrap top and bottom securely).
  • Target high-dwell transit spots (bus shelters, platform poles, traffic crossings).
  • Position posters at adult eye level (~5.5 feet) for optimal scan rates.
  • Ensure the QR code is smooth and flat (wrinkled QR codes are hard for phone cameras to register).

Guerrilla Target Demographics

This route crosses multiple Boston/Somerville/Cambridge clusters to maximize different student and arts segments:

  • Harvard & Davis Sq: Targets high-income, tech, and academic commuters.
  • Allston (Union Sq / B-Line): High density of BU students and musicians/creatives.
  • Fenway Art Cluster (Gardner, MassArt, MFA): The direct bullseye art-enthusiast segment.
  • Back Bay & Copley: General tourists, shoppers, and public library visitors.
  • Tufts University: Captures Medford/Somerville college campus student traffic.

Guerrilla Marketing Blueprint

Transit-Optimized, Rapid-Succession Route for 20 Poster Deployments

Total: 3h 14m 20 Locations Starts/Ends: 11 Waterhouse St

Phase 1: Somerville Launch

Stop 1: Teele Square Border

Travel: 3-minute walk from 11 Waterhouse St

Placement: Traffic light post at the Broadway and Curtis Street intersection.

Stop 2: Davis Square

Travel: 10-minute walk down Holland Street

Placement: The large metal utility control box directly outside the main Davis T Station entrance. Catches commuters heading underground.

Phase 2: Harvard & Allston Pipeline

Stop 3: Harvard Art Museum

Travel: 15 minutes via Red Line (Davis to Harvard) + 5-minute walk to Quincy St

Placement: The crosswalk button pole directly across from the museum entrance. Targets the art-enthusiast demographic.

Stop 4: Eliot Street Bus Stop (Harvard Sq)

Travel: 5-minute walk back across Harvard Yard

Placement: On the exterior metal frame of the bus shelter. High dwell time for people waiting for the bus.

Stop 5: Union Square (Allston)

Travel: 12 minutes via the 66 Bus from Harvard Square to Union Sq/Brighton Ave stop

Placement: Light pole near the main Cambridge Street and Brighton Ave convergence, right where pedestrians wait to cross.

Stop 6: Harvard Ave B-Line Platform

Travel: 5-minute walk down Harvard Ave

Placement: The traffic light post directly on the concrete pedestrian street island. Visible to commuters waiting for the B-Line train.

Phase 3: Fenway Art Cluster

Stop 7: Kenmore T Stop

Travel: 10 minutes via Green Line B-Branch (Harvard Ave to Kenmore)

Placement: A utility box right outside the main bus loop/subway entrance.

Stop 8: Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum

Travel: 12-minute walk down Brookline Ave and Evans Way

Placement: Street sign pole on the sidewalk perimeter facing Evans Way park, capturing the museum queue crowd.

Stop 9: MassArt

Travel: 3-minute walk over to Huntington Ave

Placement: The crosswalk pole directly in front of the main MassArt campus building. Targets design and art students.

Stop 10: Museum of Fine Arts (MFA)

Travel: 4-minute walk down Huntington Ave

Placement: Light post immediately adjacent to the MFA Green Line E-Branch stop.

Phase 4: Back Bay & Downtown

Stop 11: Boston Public Library (Copley)

Travel: 8 minutes via Green Line E-Branch (MFA to Copley)

Placement: Heavy traffic control box at the busy corner of Boylston Street and Dartmouth Street.

Stop 12: Boston Common (Arlington Side)

Travel: 5-minute walk down Boylston Street

Placement: A perimeter utility pole right at the corner of the park, directly across from the Arlington T exit.

Stop 13: Boston Common (Park Street Side)

Travel: 8-minute walk across Tremont Street side of the Common

Placement: The metal traffic control box situated right next to the historic Park Street T station kiosks.

Stop 14: Post Office Square

Travel: 8-minute walk down Winter/Summer Street into the Financial District

Placement: A perimeter streetlamp post on the sidewalk bordering the central park area.

Stop 15: Christopher Columbus Waterfront Park

Travel: 8-minute walk towards Atlantic Ave and the Greenway

Placement: A prominent signpost or light pole near the park's main Atlantic Ave brick pavilion entrance.

Stop 16: Paul Revere Statue (North End)

Travel: 7-minute walk up into the North End along Hanover Street

Placement: A streetlamp post on Hanover Street directly outside the entrance to the Paul Revere Mall courtyard.

Phase 5: Tufts & Home Stretch

Stop 17: Tufts University (Lower Campus / JCC)

Travel: 20 minutes. Walk 5 mins to Haymarket, then Green Line Extension (GLX) Med-Hill branch to Tufts

Placement: A light pole directly outside the exit of the Tufts GLX station.

Stop 18: Tufts University (Upper Campus)

Travel: 5-minute walk up the campus hill

Placement: A public outdoor bulletin board or utility post near the Mayer Campus Center.

Stop 19: Nathan Tufts Park (Powderhouse Square)

Travel: 8-minute walk down College Ave to the rotary

Placement: A signpost near the main walking path entrance closest to the historic stone powder house.

Stop 20: Teele Square Approach

Travel: 8-minute walk back toward your neighborhood

Placement: A utility pole on the final stretch of your walk home to anchor the perimeter of your local neighborhood crowd.
